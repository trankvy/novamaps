Turn complex drone data into maps your crew can act on.

Nova Maps is the software arm of Hummingbird Drones — built by former BC Wildfire Service firefighters who needed mapping tools that didn’t exist on the fireline. This brand concept explores how Nova’s visual identity can better reflect the precision, clarity, and reliability that its product already delivers.

Mark Context

The spark in Nova’s existing logomark carries the right idea — a point of light emerging from data. The thermal gradient grounds it in the product’s core visual language: the color spectrum that crews see when drone imagery reveals what’s hidden beneath canopy, smoke, or darkness.

The core product moment. Raw infrared drone imagery is noise — complex, dense, and time-consuming to interpret manually. Nova’s AI processes it automatically, identifies hidden hotspots with confidence scores, and delivers a georeferenced map that ground crews can act on immediately.

“UAVs are changing mop-up by providing real, objective data that works alongside and reinforces the efforts of crews on the ground.”Matt Dargan · UAV Program Manager, Sacramento Metro Fire

That word — alongside — matters. Nova doesn’t replace the crew’s judgment. It gives them information they couldn’t see before, so they can make better decisions faster.

Why this first.

Nova publishes a new Jake post roughly every 2–3 weeks. Each one is currently built from scratch — screenshots dropped in manually, no reusable layout, no annotation system, no video integration. A repeatable template means every future post ships faster, looks consistent, and teaches more effectively.

It also sets a pattern. The same template thinking — repeatable structure, consistent containers, annotation layers, embedded media — extends to case studies, product announcements, partner spotlights, and the Learn Center. Build the system once for Jake. Then let it grow.
